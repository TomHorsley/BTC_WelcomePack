Loading A Marker

What Is It?

We use a marker to indicate to a dog (or any animal - even humans!) that a reward is coming. By linking a specific word or noise (clicker) to the reward, we can then use the marker and a reward every time that our dog does something we want to encourage (more on this later).

Be careful in your choice of marker, because everytime you use it your dog will expect a reward (at least to start with) - and if they don't get it then the link will be broken and you will have to start again.

A good choice for a marker word is "YES" said very enthusiastically, but some people find "GOOD" (also said very enthusiasticaly) to be better - as we have been trained as humans over the years to automatically say "Good Boy" or "Good Girl" when are dogs are pleasing us. I personally tend to avoid clickers as I find the noise can be a bit scary to rescue dogs, and even if you have forgetten your clicker you can still say "YES"!

This exercise is used to link the marker word (or sound) you have chosen to your chosen reward. You will know when your dog has made the link because as soon as you say that word or click the clicker then your dog will be looking at you expecting their reward.

Daily Training: Practice at the start of every training session, repeat ten to twelve times to reinforce the link.

Steps To Success

Say the chosen marker - or click your clicker. Reward your dog. Repeat until your dog expects their reward every time you say the word or click your clicker.
