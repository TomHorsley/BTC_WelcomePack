DON'T PANIC.

Your dog needs you to be calm and patient to get safe.

1. Immediate Action at the Scene

Stay put: Many dogs return to the spot they were lost from, often at dusk or night. If you drove there, keep starting the engine - they know your car's sound - and leave the boot/door open.

Guided by Scent: Leave out your worn clothing or bedding. They need to find your scent, not their own. Emptying your vacuum contents in the area also helps.

2. Essential Notifications

Notify the microchip database, dog warden, local rescues, stray pounds, vets, and Leesa immediately.

Emergency Helplines:
  • Network Rail: 03457114141 (Dogs are drawn to tracks)
  • Highways Agency: 0300 123 5000

3. If You See Your Dog

DO NOT CHASE: This triggers survival mode. Your dog may not recognize you.

Calming Signals: Sit on the floor, avoid eye contact, and throw your socks toward them to get your scent out. Sing or hum quietly and let them work out it's you.

4. Search & Posters

Use posters with photos and your number. State clearly: "REPORT SIGHTINGS ONLY—DO NOT CHASE". A chased dog is at risk of being forced into unsafe decisions or killed.

Contact Drone SAR on Facebook for volunteer tracking assistance.

⚠️ Scammer Warning

Never hand over money to anyone claiming they have your dog or demanding payment for search services. Voluntary groups never charge.

Prevention & Trackers

Ensure your harness is escape-proof (avoid Julius K9 for panicking dogs). For tracking, use GPS (Tractive or Pitpat) rather than Bluetooth tags like AirTags.
