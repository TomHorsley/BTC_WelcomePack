"Focus" / "Watch Me"

What Is It?

A simple exercise to encourage your dog to look at you for encouragement and decision making. Eventually, if your dog is uncertain of what is needed it will look to you for a decision.

Daily Training: 5-15 minutes a day, up to three or four times a day.

Steps To Success

We will assume that you are using a treat as a reward. Start the exercise by showing your dog the treat, maybe let them have a taste of one.

Hold the treat up near your face.

As soon as your dog looks at your face use the marker, wait a second, and then give your dog the treat (mix and match which hand gives the treat).

Repeat until as soon as you hold your hand near your face you dog looks at you.

Once your dog is doing this consistently you can start to add the cue word “Watch Me!” or "Focus" depending on which one you want to use - but be consistent on the cue word.
