Medication

All the dogs have blood tests, non-obligatory for import, and are vaccinated and treated for worms, ticks and fleas before they are adopted. However, sometimes there is a necessity/precaution to send medication, such as additional antibiotics, which can be perfectly normal for dogs of all walks of life. A state vet stamps a certificate that they can travel. Sometimes your dog will arrive with the remainder of some medication to take.

If any medication arrives with your dog this will have been fully communicated to you prior to arrival and arranged with your consent.

Remember these are dogs that, prior to being rescued, have never been to a veterinary clinic. We do this not because import regulations require us to, but because we go above and beyond in the interests of both owner & dog. You may be sent a general guidance on this if necessary.
